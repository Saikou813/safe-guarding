Check Privacy

Authoritarian regimes and Big Tech corporations often operate as two sides of the same coin in a system known as surveillance capitalism. Regimes exploit technologies like facial recognition and AI to track citizens and weaponize personal data against critics. Big Tech platforms harvest vast amounts of data, creating "digital dossiers" that are often accessible to governments through legal mandates or covert backdoors. Breaches and Manipulation: By 2026, new regulations like the EU's Chat Control have sparked fears of warrantless mass surveillance through mandatory scanning of private messages.

Our Mission

To defend freedom, citizens must move beyond passive consumption and adopt a proactive defensestrategy: Adopt Privacy-Enhancing Technologies (PETs):Use Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) to mask IP addresses and Signal for end-to-end encrypted communication. Data Sovereignty: Limit personal data sharing and use privacy-oriented search engines like DuckDuckGo, Brave and Tor that do not track search history. Advocacy: Support comprehensive data protection laws, such as India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA), which grant individuals rights to access, rectify, and erase their data. The survival of a free society depends on the collective effort of citizens, civil society, and responsible technology firms to build an open, secure, and private internet.
